Reference

What is “slop”?

Slop is internet shorthand for low-effort, AI-generated content published at scale — articles, comments, reviews, and social posts churned out by language models with little or no human editing.

The term emerged in 2024 as LLM-generated text began flooding search results, product reviews, LinkedIn feeds, and news sites. Unlike thoughtful AI-assisted writing, slop is characterised by its formulaic structure, hollow confidence, and lack of genuine insight.

SlopTotal doesn’t judge all AI use. Humans editing AI drafts, using AI for brainstorming, or collaborating with AI tools is not slop. What we detect is the telltale fingerprint of text that was generated wholesale and published without meaningful human involvement.

Common slop signals

  • Formulaic phrases “In today’s rapidly evolving landscape”, “It’s important to note”, “delve into”
  • Rigid structure Numbered lists, “Firstly / Secondly / Moreover” stacking, five-paragraph essay format
  • Hedging & filler Excessive qualifiers, hollow conclusions, saying a lot while conveying little
  • Statistical fingerprints Low perplexity, uniform burstiness, predictable token distributions

Reading Your Score

0–25
Likely Human Low AI signals across engines. The text shows patterns consistent with human authorship.
25–50
Probably Human Minor signals detected. Could be formal writing, AI-assisted editing, or a false positive on polished text.
50–65
Mixed / Uncertain Engines disagree. May be AI-assisted, heavily edited AI output, or formal human writing that triggers classifiers.
65–80

80–100
Almost Certainly AI Broad consensus across engines. The text has clear statistical and linguistic markers of unedited AI output.
